Health Problems in South Africa

6% of the population suffers from diabetes 5 million more suffer from pre-diabetes (3.5 million people) Diabetes and Heart disease are among the highest 3 causes of death in South Africa. Lack of sufficient physical activity and unhealthy eating habits are the main reasons these two health issues are so prominent in South Africa. They more problematic in low income households and poverty-stricken areas where people are not aware of the health dangers and/or do not have the finances to support themselves if diagnosed. Research shows that physical inactivity increases the risk of a heart attack or stroke by 50% and even more likely for someone diagnosed with diabetes. Unfortunately, most underprivileged schools in south Africa don't generally accommodate outdoor activities and physical education. Poverty and Academic Potential

55.5% of south Africans are living in poverty (30.5 million people). 51% of the South African youth (18-21 years of age) claimed that they did not have the financial means to pay tuition 18% were not attending school because of lack of academic performance This is evident where the poorest of learners depend on dysfunctional public schooling with unreliable teachers in an environment which generally lacks discipline and safety. As a result, many of these learners achieve poor outcomes. Moreover, gangsterism and violence are prominent in the township and poorer household areas. This affects children's self-confidence, self-assurance and directs their attention and emotions to subjects that are not inducive to learning. This is alarming to South Africans as this deprives individuals of the necessary skills and marks to get a job, contributing to the increasing unemployment rates and crime in order to support families. This issue continues to prevail not only because of the lack of authority and education standards of some of the teachers in these schools, but also the students fear and lack of self-confidence.

Leadership Figures

between 61.8% and 64.1% of South African children grow up in households without a father. The sources of most of the problems faced in South Africa are a result of lack of great leadership figures. South African leaders have created the "personal rule paradigm" where they have treated their position for personal gain and accumulation of wealth. Their influence as leaders in politics and institutions paints a faulty definition of what it means to be a person with power. This is alarming as it imposes the mindset personal gain rather than lead by example. According to Drescher, there are seven things that a child needs that they get from having a positive relationship with their father; Security, significance, acceptance, love, praise, discipline and God. This lack of good leadership prevails as it is in a vicious cycle where children are not being taught the true meaning of servant leadership and are not receiving fatherly leadership.
